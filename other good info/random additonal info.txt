2.2. Level Encoding Analysis
The item's level appears to be encoded in a specific byte, but the encoding is not straightforward.

Hot Slugger Level Progression (Index 6 Byte):
The byte at index 6 appears to control the level. The first half of the byte jumps by two for every 16 levels, while the second half increments normally.

Level Range	Hex Range	Binary Prefix
1-15	0x11 - 0x1F	0001xxxx
16-31	0x30 - 0x3F	0011xxxx
32-47	0x50 - 0x5F	0101xxxx
48-50	0x70 - 0x72	0111xxxx

Export to Sheets
Sniper Level Encoding Issues:
Analysis of sniper rifle levels shows inconsistencies, suggesting that some bits may cross byte borders or that there is error correction involved when loading items. For example, a Level 34 sniper code had a level marker that appeared to be 18, while a Level 18 sniper's marker appeared as 50. This indicates a more complex encoding system than simple integer values.
